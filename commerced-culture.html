Commerce(d) Culture

Commerce(d) Culture is a study of the consumer culture displayed in various Vietnamese grocery stores across the United States; it explores the intersection of consumption and cultural practices in Vietnamese diasporic communities. From Little Saigon in Southern California to Philadelphia and smaller towns of rural Illinois, these stores range from local corner shops to larger multi-aisle supermarkets, attracting thousands to millions of shoppers of cultural nostalgia or exploration. The stores exist not only as a place of transaction but also a celebration of intercultural communities, an exhibition of the immigrants’ experience, and a construct of nationhood. The series includes photographs from four different Vietnamese-own grocery stores from various parts of the United States and snippets of writing serve as a narration device leading the viewer through the series and provide an additional layer of personal sentiments and intimacy to the traditional use of documentary photography.

For many customers, shopping in these stores fulfills a craving for more than just items on the shelves. The goods and decorations carried in the stores portray a construct of nationhood and nostalgia of motherland for members of Vietnamese diasporic communities. Immigrant grocery stores are where members of diasporic communities can shop for the ingredients that they use in their everyday cooking and have the privilege of doing a quick grocery run without the constant reminder that they are aliens in the communal spaces they inhabit.
