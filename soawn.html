Story of a Wounded Nation

April 30th, 1975 marked the end of the American War in Vietnam and the start of a transition period to the formal reunification of Vietnam into the Socialist Republic of Vietnam. The date has many names. For many Vietnamese people in the mainland, it is Liberation Day. For many others, it is called National Day of Shame, National Day of Resentment, Day we Lost the Country, or Black April. What thought to be a celebration has created a separation.

Many stories were told about Vietnam, most of which end on April the 30th. It is a familiar narrative about a country that successfully fought off much stronger enemies. But there is much more to that narrative: the pain, and the sacrifice, physically, mentally, and culturally, to get to where we are today, names we have been called, the language that we have lost, and a divided nation of people. We patch ourselves as we go, picking up the pieces that we have lost. We are fixing the country, but we have not yet fixed our souls.
