The Fragile Present

Photography has always been a way for me to hang on to the moment. It is hard for me to accept the fact that once a second passes, it is gone. I am scared of the change of time, or the future.

I read in an essay from Carl Becker that the idea of the “present” that we have always been holding in our mind is just a combination of what we know of our past and what we anticipate of the future. Yet it seems so joyful to say “Let’s just live in the moment.”

At times, the present sounds vast and unending, so much that you find yourself drowning in it, and a second just lasts forever. But once it passes, it becomes nothing more than a dot on our timeline. Every time we look at someone, we are looking at an infinite amount of their possibilities, their past hopes and dreams, the moments they have lost.

With this project, I am paying tribute to all of the “presents” that have passed through me. Some left an imprint on my brain, most didn’t; but they all helped to create the person that I am today.
